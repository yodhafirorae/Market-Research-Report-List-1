<p><p>The antibiotics drugs market is categorized into various types based on their chemical structure and mechanism of action. Phenicols inhibit bacterial protein synthesis, while quinolones target DNA replication. Macrolides also interfere with protein synthesis but differ in structure. Sulfonamides act as competitive inhibitors of bacterial metabolism. Aminoglycosides are effective against Gram-negative bacteria by disrupting protein synthesis, whereas tetracyclines inhibit protein synthesis across a broad spectrum. Beta-lactams, including penicillin, disrupt cell wall synthesis. Other categories encompass diverse agents used for infection management.</p></p>
