<p><p>The foodborne pathogen detection kit market includes various types, primarily focusing on PCR (Polymerase Chain Reaction) and ELISA (Enzyme-Linked Immunosorbent Assay). PCR is a molecular technique that amplifies DNA from pathogens, enabling rapid and precise detection. In contrast, ELISA is an immunological method that identifies pathogens through antibodies, offering a sensitive and relatively simple approach. Both methods are crucial for ensuring food safety by detecting contaminants in various food products, thus helping prevent foodborne illnesses.</p></p>
