<p><p>The suture anchor system market comprises two primary types: absorbable and non-absorbable suture anchors. Absorbable suture anchors are designed to dissolve over time, eliminating the need for removal and reducing long-term complications. They are often used in soft tissue repair, particularly in pediatric patients. Non-absorbable suture anchors, on the other hand, provide enduring stability and durability, making them suitable for long-term fixation in various orthopedic and surgical applications. This differentiation caters to varying clinical needs and patient considerations.</p></p>
